▸ PhD (Physics) · MLIPs / DFT / RT‑TDDFT

Building fast, accurate methods for atomistic simulation — from quantum embedding to GNN potentials.

I’m a postdoctoral researcher at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), working on graph neural network (GNN) interatomic potentials for thin‑film growth (CVD/PVD), and on first‑principles workflows (DFT, AIMD, NEB) for atomic‑scale mechanisms. Previously, I developed DFT‑based quantum embedding methods in TURBOMOLE during my PhD at FSU Jena (summa cum laude).

About

Short, editable snapshot (academia + software + outreach).

What I do now

Postdoctoral researcher at IISc (Chemical Engineering), jointly advised by Prof. Ananth Govind Rajan and Prof. Sudeep Punnathanam, focusing on GNN-based machine‑learned interatomic potentials and first‑principles workflows for thin‑film growth.

What I did before

PhD in Physics at Friedrich Schiller University Jena (Germany), developing DFT‑based quantum embedding methods coupled with WFT and real‑time TDDFT, with contributions to TURBOMOLE.

Research focus
